1 Introduction

DNA methylation is a kind of modification that exists in almost all higher organisms and has been widely studied. In prokaryotes, it mainly occurs on the carbon atoms of cytosine and adenine; in eukaryotes, methylation mainly refers to 5-methylcytosine (5mC), which is formed by adding the methyl groups to the 5’C position of cytosine under the action of DNA methyltransferase (DNMT) (Vertino PM, 1996). In mammalian cells, methylation mainly occurs on cytosine of CG dinucleotides (Goldberg AD, 2007), while in plant cells, there's a large proportion of non-CG (CHH, CHG, H represents A, C, T) methylation.(Jackson JP, 2002). Although gene modification does not change the gene sequence, it still participates in the expression regulation of various genes and regulates life activities of many cells, including cell differentiation, tissue-specific gene expression, genomic imprinting, X chromosome inactivation, etc.

It is generally believed that DNA is the carrier of biological genetic information, and the Central Dogma further indicates that mRNA connects DNA and protein, plays an important role between genetic information and proteins that perform biological functions. Research on mRNA has become an important and powerful tool for studying growth and development, stress tolerance, and biological evolution.

Most studies suggest that high DNA methylation usually inhibits gene expression, while demethylation can lead to gene re-expression (Jidi Xu, Shasha Zhou, Xiaoqing Gong. 2017); abnormal DNA methylation can affect expression of related genes, leading to developmental abnormalities, tumors, and other diseases.

With the rapid development of second-generation sequencing in recent years, second-generation sequencing has the huge advantages of high-throughput, accuracy and cheapness, laying the foundation for the joint application of multi-omics and multi-platforms, and becoming an important means of studying epigenetic regulation. At the same time, the joint analysis of multi-omics has also become the highlight of many studies, and also the trend of research. The combination of methylation and transcriptome means a multi-level and multi-dimension explaination of a biological event, and a multi-angle demonstration of biological issues. It is widely used in disease research, biological traits and other fields (Maojun wan1, Daojun yuan, et al. 2015, Maojun Wang, Daojun yuan et al. 2015, Jidi Xu, Shasha Zhou, Xiaoqing Gong et al.2017).

Where Rich factor refers to the ratio of the number of overlapping genes enriched in the pathway to the number of annotated genes. The larger the Rich factor, the greater the degree of enrichment. Qvalue is the Pvalue after multiple hypothesis test correction. The value of Qvalue is [0,1]. The enrichment will be more significant if the q-value is close to 0. The most significant enriched 20 pathways are presented in the scatter plot. If there are less than 20 pathways, all the pathways will be presented. In addition, we also provide a web interface that can display ovlerlapping genes through KEGG's metabolic pathway map.
