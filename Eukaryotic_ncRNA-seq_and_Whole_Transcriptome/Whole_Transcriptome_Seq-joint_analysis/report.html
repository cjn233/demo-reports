1.2 Candidate target gene enrichment

miRNA regulates mRNA by post-transcriptional regulation, in which miRNA inhibits or even silences its target mRNA so that the mRNA could no longer be translated into a protein. Here we name the target genes of differentially expressed miRNA as “candidate target genes”. To explore possible miRNA-mRNA interactions, we select 3 types of candidate target genes for GO and KEGG enrichment analysis and further network construction:

(1) Differentially expressed miRNA and differentially expressed mRNA: we take the intersection of target genes of differentially expressed miRNA and genes of differentially expressed mRNA.

(2) Down-regulated miRNA and up-regulated mRNA.

(3) Up-regulated miRNA and down-regulated mRNA.

Note: Below shows the enrichment analysis result of (1). For the rest results, please refer to the folder result/*miRNA_mRNA/*vs*/.

2.1 Correlation analysis of lncRNA and mRNA

LncRNA regulates the expression of target mRNA by co-location or co-expression. The lncRNA and mRNA correlation analysis is based on the intersection analysis of target genes of differentially expressed lncRNA and differentially expressed mRNA. When target genes of lncRNA are also significantly differentially expressed mRNA, the differentially expressed mRNA is more likely to be regulated directly or indirectly by that lncRNA. For fewer than 5 samples, we compare target genes of lncRNA by co-location with differentially expressed mRNA. For more than 5 samples, we compare target genes of lncRNA by both co-location and co-expression with differentially expressed mRNA. The intersection of differentially expressed lncRNA’s target genes and differentially expressed mRNA is shown in a Venn diagram.

2.3. Regulatory networks of lncRNA, miRNA, and mRNA

lncRNA has a wide range of regulatory functions. It could not only directly regulate the DNA transcription and translation of RNA, but also indirectly regulate gene expression. This is because lncRNA harbours miRNA binding sites and could bind to miRNA like a sponge to competitively keep miRNA from regulating its downstream target genes. Based on the ceRNA hypothesis, we filter out lncRNA and genes targeted by the same miRNA, and construct lncRNA-miRNA-gene networks to reveal the expression regulation mechanism of lncRNA at the whole transcriptome level (Figure 4.1).
