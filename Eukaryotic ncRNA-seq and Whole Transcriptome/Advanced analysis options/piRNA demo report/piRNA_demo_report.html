Small RNA Sequencing Project Workflow

Small RNA is a special kind of molecule in organisms that induces gene silencing and plays an important role in the regulation of cell growth, gene transcription, and translation. Piwi-interacting RNA (piRNA) is one class of small non-coding RNA molecules, forming RNA-protein complexes through interactions with piwi-subfamily proteins, which are mostly involved in the epigenetic and post-transcriptional silencing of transposons. Small RNA digitalization analysis, based on mechanism of SBS (sequencing by synthesis), has the benefits of small sample requirements, high throughput, high accuracy, and a simple automatic platform.

Next-generation sequencing can obtain millions of small RNA sequence tags in one shot, comprehensively identifying small RNAs of a certain species under certain status. predicting novel RNA and giving differential expression profile between samples. This expression can be a powerful tool for researching small RNA functions.

Small RNA sequencing projects are carried out as follows:

They are distinct from microRNA (miRNA) in size (26–31 nt rather than 21–24 nt), lack of sequence conservation, increased complexity, and independence of Dicer for biogenesis at least in animals. (Plant Dcl2 may play a role in rasi/piRNA biogenesis.)

2.1.5 Enrichment Analysis of piRNA-generating Genes

piRNA-generating loci are often found in large genomic clusters (called “piRNA clusters”) from which long primary transcripts are transcribed and individual piRNAs processed. GO enrichment analysis and KEGG enrichment analysis are performed on the piRNA-generating genes.

2.1.5.1 GO Enrichment Analysis

GO is the abbreviation of Gene Ontology (http://www.geneontology.org/), which is a major bioinformatics classification system to unify the presentation of gene properties across all species. The GO enrichment analysis is performed by GOSeq (Young et al, 2010), based on the Wallenius non-central hypergeometric distribution, the mechanism of which is estimate the probability of candidate gene enrichment by the preference of gene length. It includes three main branches: cellular compares, molecular function and biological process. GO terms with padj < 0.05 are significant enrichment.

2.1.5.2 KEGG Enrichment Analysis of piRNA-generating Genes

The interactions of multiple genes may be involved in certain biological functions. KEGG (Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes, http://www.kegg.jp/) is a collection of manually curated databases containing resource of genomic, biological-pathway and disease information (Kanehisa, 2008). Pathway enrichment analysis identifies significantly enriched metabolic pathways or signal transduction pathways associated with differentially expressed genes, comparing to the whole genome background. The mechanism of KEGG enrichment can be:


N = the number of all genes with KEGG annotation

n = the number of target gene candidates in N

M = the number of all genes annotated to a certain pathway

m = the number of target gene candidates in M


KEGG terms with padj < 0.05 are significant enrichment. KEGG analysis reveals the main pathways with which the target gene candidates are involved. Result Directory: Results/14.Enrichment/*/KEGGenrichment/*.

2.2 piRNA Cluster Analysis

Generally, piRNA clusters are genetic elements with length ranging from 20 to 100 kb and density ranging from 40 to 4000. The piRNA cluster is primarily transcribed forming a long single-stranded precursor, which is sequentially processed to be a mature piRNA with length of 26-32 nt.
